* Understanding Org-Mode Export

Before we export, let's understand how it works.

** The Export Dispatcher

Org-mode has a unified export interface:

1. Open your manuscript
2. Press ~C-c C-e~ (org-export-dispatch)
3. You'll see the export menu:

#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
Org Export Dispatcher
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

[b] Export to buffer       [l] Export to LaTeX
[h] Export to HTML         [o] Export to ODT
[p] Export to PDF          [t] Export to plain text
[m] Export to Markdown     [e] Export to EPUB (if installed)

[q] Quit
#+END_EXAMPLE

*This is your export command center!*

** Understanding Export Options

Each format has variations:

| Key Sequence | What It Does                                      |
|--------------+---------------------------------------------------|
| ~C-c C-e l o~  | Export to LaTeX and open file                     |
| ~C-c C-e l l~  | Export to LaTeX (don't open)                      |
| ~C-c C-e l p~  | Export to PDF (LaTeX → PDF)                       |
| ~C-c C-e h h~  | Export to HTML (single file)                      |
| ~C-c C-e h o~  | Export to HTML and open in browser                |
| ~C-c C-e o o~  | Export to ODT and open in LibreOffice/Word        |

*Pattern*: First letter = format, second letter = action

** Export Scope

You can export:
- **Whole buffer**: Default, exports everything
- **Subtree**: Just current section (with ~C-c C-e C-s~ prefix)
- **Visible only**: Only expanded headings (with ~C-c C-e C-v~ prefix)

*For manuscripts*: Usually export whole buffer.

** What Gets Exported?

Org-mode respects:
- ~:ignore:~ tag on headings → Heading excluded (but content included)
- ~:noexport:~ tag on headings → Heading and content excluded
- ~#+OPTIONS: toc:nil~ → No table of contents
- ~#+EXCLUDE_TAGS: noexport~ → Tags to exclude

*Your template already has these configured!*

* The Scene Break Macro

Scene breaks are crucial in fiction - they show time/location/POV changes within a chapter.

** The Problem

You write in your manuscript:
#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
Alex walked out. The door closed behind them.

[SCENE BREAK - Later that evening]

Sam sat in the coffee shop, waiting.
#+END_EXAMPLE

But each export format needs different scene breaks:
- **Print (PDF)**: Extra vertical space (3 blank lines)
- **Word (ODT)**: Three asterisks centered (***) or vertical space
- **HTML**: Multiple line breaks
- **EPUB**: Proper structural break

**Manually changing this for each format is tedious!**

** The Solution: Scene Break Macro

~emacs-writing~ provides a macro that automatically converts to the right format.

*** How It Works

1. Your template has this line at the top:
   #+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
   #+MACRO: scene-break SCENE-BREAK
   #+END_EXAMPLE

2. In your manuscript, use:
   #+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
   {{{scene-break}}}
   #+END_EXAMPLE

3. When exporting:
   - LaTeX: Converts to ~\vspace{...}~ (vertical space)
   - HTML: Converts to ~<br><br><br>~ (line breaks)
   - ODT: Converts to line breaks
   - ASCII/Markdown: Converts to ~***~

** Using Scene Breaks

Replace manual scene breaks with the macro:

*** Before (Manual)
#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
Alex walked out.

***

Sam sat waiting.
#+END_EXAMPLE

*** After (Macro)
#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
Alex walked out.

{{{scene-break}}}

Sam sat waiting.
#+END_EXAMPLE

** Scene Break Example

Here's a real manuscript snippet:

#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
*** Scene 1 - Morning
:PROPERTIES:
:PoV: Alex
:END:

Alex stumbled into the kitchen, eyes half-closed. Coffee. Need coffee.

The machine gurgled to life. While it worked, Alex stared out the window
at the gray morning. Another Monday.

{{{scene-break}}}

Twenty minutes later, coffee in hand, Alex felt almost human. The first
sip was always the best—that moment when the fog started to lift.

{{{scene-break}}}

By the second cup, Alex had a plan for the day. Well, the outline of a
plan. More of a vague direction than an actual plan, but it was a start.
#+END_EXAMPLE

*Three scene breaks* showing:
1. Time jump (20 minutes)
2. Mood shift (foggy → clearer)

When exported, each format gets appropriate breaks automatically!

** Try It Now! (2 minutes)

1. Open your manuscript
2. Find a scene with a time/location jump
3. Add: ~{{{scene-break}}}~ between sections
4. Save the file
5. We'll export it in the next sections!

* Exporting to PDF (via LaTeX)

PDF is the gold standard for print and submission to publishers.

** Requirements

- LaTeX distribution installed (TeX Live, MacTeX, or MiKTeX)
- Verify: ~pdflatex --version~ in terminal

** Step 1: Check LaTeX Configuration

Your template has LaTeX settings:

#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
#+LATEX_CLASS: book
#+LATEX_HEADER:
#+LATEX_HEADER_EXTRA:
#+END_EXAMPLE

*Default*: Uses "book" class (appropriate for novels)

** Step 2: Export to PDF

1. Open your manuscript (~novel.org~ or ~story.org~)
2. Press ~C-c C-e~ (export dispatcher)
3. Press ~l~ (LaTeX)
4. Press ~p~ (PDF)
5. Wait for compilation (10-30 seconds)
6. PDF appears in same directory!

*Full sequence*: ~C-c C-e l p~

** What You Get

The PDF includes:
- Title page (from ~#+TITLE~ and ~#+AUTHOR~)
- Automatically formatted chapters and scenes
- Proper page breaks
- Scene breaks as vertical space
- Professional book-like appearance

** Customizing PDF Output

*** Removing Table of Contents

Add to your manuscript:
#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
#+OPTIONS: toc:nil
#+END_EXAMPLE

*** Changing Chapter Numbering

Add:
#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
#+OPTIONS: num:nil
#+END_EXAMPLE

Now chapters show as "Chapter 1" without numbers on sections.

*** Custom LaTeX Class

For specific publisher requirements:

#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
#+LATEX_CLASS: article        (shorter works)
#+LATEX_CLASS: book           (novels - default)
#+LATEX_CLASS: memoir         (extensive customization)
#+END_EXAMPLE

*** Adding LaTeX Packages

For special formatting:

#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
#+LATEX_HEADER: \usepackage{geometry}
#+LATEX_HEADER: \geometry{margin=1in}
#+LATEX_HEADER: \usepackage{setspace}
#+LATEX_HEADER: \doublespacing
#+END_EXAMPLE

This creates standard manuscript format (1-inch margins, double-spaced).

** Common PDF Issues

*** "LaTeX command not found"

*Problem*: LaTeX not installed

*Solution*: Install TeX Live (Linux/Mac) or MiKTeX (Windows)

*** "PDF not created"

*Problem*: LaTeX compilation error

*Solution*:
1. Check ~*Org PDF LaTeX Output*~ buffer for errors
2. Look for ~! ~ in the output (error marker)
3. Common issues: special characters, missing packages

*** PDF Looks Wrong

*Problem*: LaTeX class or settings mismatch

*Solution*:
1. Try ~#+LATEX_CLASS: article~ for simpler layout
2. Remove custom LaTeX headers temporarily
3. Export a minimal test document first

** Try It Now! (3 minutes)

1. Open your manuscript
2. ~C-c C-e l p~ (export to PDF)
3. Wait for compilation
4. Open the PDF
5. Check: title page, chapters, scene breaks
6. Note any formatting you want to change

* Exporting to ODT (Word Format)

ODT (OpenDocument Text) opens in LibreOffice, Google Docs, and Microsoft Word.

** Requirements

- None! ODT export is built into Emacs
- To open: LibreOffice (free) or Microsoft Word

** Step 1: Export to ODT

1. Open your manuscript
2. ~C-c C-e~ (export dispatcher)
3. ~o~ (ODT)
4. ~o~ (export and open)
5. File opens in your default ODT editor

*Full sequence*: ~C-c C-e o o~

** What You Get

The ODT file includes:
- Formatted headings (chapters as Heading 1, scenes as Heading 2)
- Scene breaks (vertical space or ***)
- Metadata (title, author)
- Editable paragraphs

*Important*: ODT is fully editable - you can make final tweaks in LibreOffice/Word!

** Customizing ODT Output

*** Using a Custom Style Template

Professional publishers often require specific formatting. You can use an ODT template:

1. Create your formatting in LibreOffice (fonts, margins, headers)
2. Save as template: ~my-manuscript.ott~
3. In your org file, add:
   #+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
   #+ODT_STYLES_FILE: "~/org-scribe/templates/my-manuscript.ott"
   #+END_EXAMPLE
4. Export - your formatting is applied!

*** Manuscript Format Template

Standard manuscript format:
- 12pt Courier or Times New Roman
- Double-spaced
- 1-inch margins
- Header with page numbers

Create this once in LibreOffice, save as .ott, reuse for all manuscripts!

** Common ODT Uses

*** Beta Reader Format

Beta readers often prefer Word format:
1. Export to ODT
2. Add comments instructions
3. Share via email/Google Drive
4. They edit/comment in Word
5. You incorporate feedback in org-mode

*** Publisher Submission

Many publishers accept .docx or .odt:
1. Check publisher guidelines
2. Export to ODT
3. Open in LibreOffice/Word
4. Final formatting tweaks
5. Save as .docx if required
6. Submit!

*** Agent Submission

Literary agents typically want:
- Times New Roman, 12pt
- Double-spaced
- Header with name/title/page number

Use ODT template for this!

** Try It Now! (2 minutes)

1. Open manuscript
2. ~C-c C-e o o~ (export to ODT and open)
3. File opens in LibreOffice/Word
4. Check formatting
5. Try editing a paragraph (it's fully editable!)
6. Close without saving (we'll keep working in org)

* Exporting to HTML

HTML is perfect for web publishing, blogs, and digital sharing.

** Requirements

- None! HTML export is built into Emacs
- To view: Any web browser

** Step 1: Export to HTML

1. Open manuscript
2. ~C-c C-e~ (export dispatcher)
3. ~h~ (HTML)
4. ~h~ (export to file) OR ~o~ (export and open in browser)

*Full sequence*: ~C-c C-e h o~ (open in browser)

** What You Get

The HTML file includes:
- Formatted headings (<h1>, <h2>, etc.)
- Paragraphs with proper spacing
- Scene breaks as line breaks
- Navigation (if table of contents enabled)
- CSS-styled presentation

** Customizing HTML Output

*** Adding a Stylesheet

For professional appearance:

#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
#+HTML_HEAD: <link rel="stylesheet" type="text/css" href="style.css"/>
#+END_EXAMPLE

Create ~style.css~ with:
#+BEGIN_SRC css
body {
    font-family: Georgia, serif;
    line-height: 1.6;
    max-width: 40em;
    margin: 2em auto;
    padding: 0 1em;
}

h1, h2 {
    font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', sans-serif;
}

p {
    text-align: justify;
    margin-bottom: 1em;
}
#+END_SRC

Now your HTML looks professional!

*** HTML5 vs XHTML

For modern browsers:
#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
#+HTML_DOCTYPE: html5
#+END_EXAMPLE

For EPUB compatibility:
#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
#+HTML_DOCTYPE: xhtml
#+END_EXAMPLE

*** Table of Contents

Enable/disable TOC:
#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
#+OPTIONS: toc:t      (show TOC)
#+OPTIONS: toc:nil    (hide TOC)
#+OPTIONS: toc:2      (TOC depth = 2 levels)
#+END_EXAMPLE

** Common HTML Uses

*** Web Serial

Publish your novel chapter-by-chapter online:
1. Export each chapter to HTML
2. Add navigation links
3. Upload to website/blog
4. Share links with readers

*** Digital Preview

Give readers a preview:
1. Export first 3 chapters to HTML
2. Add "buy the book" link at end
3. Share via email/website
4. Beautiful, accessible format

*** Archive Version

Keep a readable web version:
- Export complete manuscript to HTML
- Store in Dropbox/cloud
- Accessible from any device
- No special software needed

* Customizing Export Settings

Your template includes extensive export configuration. Let's understand it!

** Location of Export Settings

In your manuscript, find the "Export config" section (usually at the bottom):

#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
* Export config :ignore:noexport:
#+END_EXAMPLE

*Note*: This section is tagged ~:noexport:~ so it doesn't appear in exports!

** General Export Options

#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
#+OPTIONS: ':t *:t -:t ::t <:t H:3 \n:nil ^:t arch:headline author:t
#+OPTIONS: broken-links:nil c:nil creator:nil d:(not "LOGBOOK")
#+OPTIONS: date:t e:t email:nil f:t inline:t num:t p:nil pri:nil
#+OPTIONS: prop:nil stat:t tags:t tasks:t tex:t timestamp:t title:t
#+OPTIONS: toc:2 todo:t |:t
#+END_EXAMPLE

*What these mean*:

| Option      | Meaning                                |
|-------------+----------------------------------------|
| ~H:3~         | Heading depth (3 levels max)           |
| ~num:t~       | Number headings                        |
| ~toc:2~       | Table of contents (2 levels deep)      |
| ~author:t~    | Include author name                    |
| ~date:t~      | Include date                           |
| ~email:nil~   | Don't include email                    |
| ~tags:t~      | Include tags in export                 |
| ~todo:t~      | Include TODO keywords                  |

** Common Customizations

*** Remove Table of Contents

Change:
#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
#+OPTIONS: toc:nil
#+END_EXAMPLE

*** Hide TODO Keywords

Change:
#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
#+OPTIONS: todo:nil
#+END_EXAMPLE

Now scenes marked ~*** TODO Scene 3~ export as ~*** Scene 3~

*** Disable Section Numbers

Change:
#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
#+OPTIONS: num:nil
#+END_EXAMPLE

*** Include Email

Change:
#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
#+OPTIONS: email:t
#+END_EXAMPLE

* Preparing for Publisher Submission

Different publishers have different requirements. Here's how to adapt.

** Standard Manuscript Format (Shunn Format)

Most publishers request:
- Times New Roman or Courier, 12pt
- Double-spaced
- 1-inch margins all around
- Header: Last Name / TITLE / Page #
- First page: Contact info top left

*** LaTeX Configuration for Shunn Format

Add to your manuscript:

#+BEGIN_SRC org
,#+LATEX_HEADER: \usepackage{geometry}
,#+LATEX_HEADER: \geometry{margin=1in}
,#+LATEX_HEADER: \usepackage{setspace}
,#+LATEX_HEADER: \doublespacing
,#+LATEX_HEADER: \usepackage{fancyhdr}
,#+LATEX_HEADER: \pagestyle{fancy}
,#+LATEX_HEADER: \fancyhead[L]{Your Name}
,#+LATEX_HEADER: \fancyhead[C]{NOVEL TITLE}
,#+LATEX_HEADER: \fancyhead[R]{\thepage}
#+END_SRC

*** ODT Configuration for Shunn Format

1. Export to ODT
2. Open in LibreOffice
3. Format → Page Style → Headers/Footers
4. Add header with name/title/page number
5. Format → Paragraph → Line spacing → Double
6. Format → Page → Margins → 1 inch all
7. Save as template (.ott)
8. Reuse for all submissions!

** Agent Submission

Agents typically want:
- Cover letter (separate file)
- Synopsis (1-2 pages)
- First 3 chapters or 50 pages
- Standard manuscript format

*Process*:
1. Use subtree export for first 3 chapters:
   - Position cursor on Chapter 1
   - ~C-c C-e C-s~ (subtree scope)
   - ~o o~ (export to ODT)
2. Manually create cover letter
3. Manually create synopsis
4. Combine according to agent's instructions

** Self-Publishing

For Amazon KDP or similar:
- EPUB or MOBI format
- Proper cover image
- Complete metadata
- No manuscript format needed (readers don't care!)

*Process*:
1. Export to EPUB (~C-c C-e e e~)
2. Validate with EPUBCheck
3. Convert to MOBI with Calibre (if needed)
4. Upload to KDP
5. Preview with Kindle Previewer
6. Publish!

** Contest Submission

Writing contests often have strict requirements:
- Anonymous submission (no author name)
- Specific format (usually PDF)
- Page limits
- Specific margins/spacing

*Process*:
1. Temporarily remove ~#+AUTHOR~
2. Set ~#+OPTIONS: author:nil~
3. Add contest-specific LaTeX settings
4. Export to PDF
5. Verify page count
6. Submit!

* Common Export Issues and Solutions

** Problem: Export Creates Multiple Files

*Symptom*: Exporting creates ~novel.tex~, ~novel.aux~, ~novel.log~, etc.

*Cause*: LaTeX creates intermediate files

*Solution*:
- This is normal for PDF export
- Only share the .pdf file
- Delete intermediate files if desired: ~rm novel.{tex,aux,log}~

** Problem: Special Characters Break Export

*Symptom*: Export fails with error about "unknown character"

*Cause*: Some characters need escaping in LaTeX

*Solution*:
- Use org entities: ~\tilde~ for ~, ~\nbsp~ for non-breaking space
- For literal text: ~#+BEGIN_EXPORT latex~ blocks
- Check encoding: save files as UTF-8

** Problem: Scene Breaks Don't Appear

*Symptom*: Scene breaks show as "SCENE-BREAK" literally

*Cause*: Macro not defined or not working

*Solution*:
1. Check top of file has: ~#+MACRO: scene-break SCENE-BREAK~
2. Verify using ~{{{scene-break}}}~ not ~{{{scene break}}}~ (no space!)
3. Check ~writing-export.el~ is loaded: ~M-x writing-version~

** Problem: Export Shows TODO Keywords

*Symptom*: PDF/ODT shows "TODO Scene 3" instead of "Scene 3"

*Cause*: TODO export enabled

*Solution*: Add ~#+OPTIONS: todo:nil~

** Problem: Chapters Not Numbered Correctly

*Symptom*: Chapters show as "1.1" instead of "Chapter 1"

*Cause*: Heading numbering style

*Solution*:
- For LaTeX: Use ~\chapter~ instead of ~\section~
- Change heading levels: * = chapter, ** = scene
- Or disable numbering: ~#+OPTIONS: num:nil~

** Problem: Images Don't Appear in Export

*Symptom*: Images show as broken links

*Cause*: Image paths incorrect

*Solution*:
- Use relative paths: ~[[./images/map.png]]~
- Or absolute paths: ~[[file:/full/path/to/image.png]]~
- Verify images exist before exporting

** Problem: EPUB Doesn't Validate

*Symptom*: EPUBCheck reports errors

*Cause*: Invalid XHTML or missing metadata

*Solution*:
1. Set ~#+HTML_DOCTYPE: xhtml~
2. Add all required metadata (UID, Title, Author)
3. Fix reported HTML errors
4. Revalidate until clean

#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
