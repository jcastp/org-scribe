** General Description
A modern mid-sized city with a mix of old and new architecture. Urban but not
overwhelming. Has distinct neighborhoods with their own character. Public
transportation exists but most people drive. The kind of place where you can
find both chain coffee shops and local favorites.

** Geography

- Location :: East coast (implied but not specified)
- Terrain :: Relatively flat, some hills, near water
- Climate :: Four seasons, emphasis on crisp autumn mornings and harsh winter alarms
- Natural resources :: Parks, waterfront area

** Cultural Aspects

- Language :: English (American)
- Customs :: Contemporary American urban culture
- Government :: City council (not relevant to story)

** History
Not relevant to this story - contemporary setting.

** Importance in the Plot
The city itself is more of a backdrop. It's deliberately generic so readers can
project their own city onto it. The specific locations within the city (apartment,
coffee shop) are more important than the city as a whole.

* Corner Coffee Shop
:PROPERTIES:
:TYPE: Local coffee shop
:NAME: "Corner Coffee Shop" (generic, could have a name)
:ESTABLISHED: ~10 years ago
:END:

** General Description
Local independent coffee shop, not a chain. Cozy but busy in the mornings.
Counter up front, seating in back. Mix of regulars and commuters grabbing
coffee to go. The kind of place where baristas recognize regulars but there's
also always someone new.

** Layout
- Front :: Counter, register, pastry case, coffee equipment
- Back :: Small tables, a few comfortable chairs, corner booth
- Side wall :: Community board, outlet bar for laptops
- Window :: Front window with the shop name, looks out on street

** Atmosphere & Role in Story
This is where Alex and Sam's first real conversation happens. It's a transitional
space - between home and work, between night and day, between chaos and order.

The coffee shop represents routine for Sam (same time, same order every day) and
chaos for Alex (rushed, line too long, never the same time).

Later it becomes a neutral meeting ground where they talk. By the end, Alex
experiences it the way Sam does - as a peaceful morning ritual, not a stressful
rush.

** Sensory Details
- Sight :: Warm lighting, espresso machine gleaming, people in various states of awakeness
- Sound :: Espresso machine hissing, milk steaming, low conversation, music barely audible
- Smell :: Coffee beans, baked goods, slight burnt-sugar scent from caramel
- Touch :: Smooth counter, warm cup in hand, cool door handle
- Taste :: Quality coffee, better than what Alex makes at home

** Characters Associated
- Alex :: Initially rushed and stressed here
- Sam :: Regular, orders same thing, calm and collected
- Barista :: Minor character, efficient, knows Sam's order
- New Character :: Appears here in final scene, completing the circle

** Symbolic Meaning
The coffee shop is where change begins (first encounter with Sam) and where it
comes full circle (helping the new person). It's a threshold space.
