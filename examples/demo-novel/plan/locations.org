A small studio apartment that's seen better organization. Creative chaos - art supplies, design books, multiple coffee mugs in various states of use. Big window with decent light (when Alex opens the curtains). Furniture is mismatched but comfortable.

** Geography

- Location :: 2A in the apartment building
- Terrain :: Studio apartment, single main room
- Climate :: Indoor, occasionally stuffy from closed curtains
- Natural resources :: Natural light from big window (when curtains open)

** Cultural Aspects & Society

- Language :: N/A
- Customs :: Freelancer work-from-home lifestyle
- Religion :: N/A
- Government :: N/A

** History

Alex's home for several years. Has gradually become more chaotic, especially after grandmother's death 2 years ago.

** Notable Features

- **The Bed** :: Scene of the snooze button battles
- **The Desk** :: Where Alex works, usually cluttered
- **The Window** :: Curtains usually closed, light barely gets in
- **Coffee Table** :: Where the watch is discovered under mail pile
- **The Junk Drawer** :: Source of the spare notebook
- **Kitchenette** :: Along one wall, barely used beyond coffee maker
- **Closet** :: Overstuffed, source of the daily "what to wear" crisis

** Importance in the Plot

Primary setting. Where Alex's chaos is most visible. Where the transformation begins (finding the watch). By story's end, becomes more organized but retains personality. Represents Alex's internal state - chaotic at first, gradually finding balance.

** Specific Places

Main room (combined living/sleeping/working area), kitchenette, bathroom, closet.

** Atmosphere & Mood

**Morning**: Chaotic, dim (curtains closed), cluttered but colorful (art/design materials)
**Afternoon**: Creative energy
**Evening**: Exhaustion

**By story end**: More organized but retains personality - organized chaos rather than sterile minimalism.

**Sensory Details**:
- Sight :: Dim (curtains closed), cluttered but colorful (art/design materials)
- Sound :: Traffic from street below, neighbor's TV through walls, alarm buzzing
- Smell :: Stale coffee, sometimes takeout
- Touch :: Rumpled sheets, smooth phone screen, worn carpet
- Emotional feel :: Initially claustrophobic, gradually becomes cozy

* Corner Coffee Shop
:PROPERTIES:
:ID: loc-coffee-shop-001
:Type: Building
:Importance: Major
:First-appearance: Chapter 1, Scene 2
:Climate: Indoor, warm
:Population: Varies, 10-30 customers during morning rush

** General Description

Local independent coffee shop (name TBD or keep generic "Corner Coffee Shop"). Not a chain. Cozy but busy in the mornings. Established about 10 years ago. Counter up front, seating in back. Mix of regulars and commuters grabbing coffee to go. The kind of place where baristas recognize regulars but there's also always someone new.

** Geography

- Location :: Near Alex's apartment building
- Terrain :: Single-story building, corner location
- Climate :: Warm, inviting interior
- Natural resources :: Coffee, pastries

** Cultural Aspects & Society

- Language :: English
- Customs :: Coffee shop culture, regulars have "their" orders
- Religion :: N/A
- Government :: Small business

** History

Established ~10 years ago. Has become a neighborhood fixture. Outlasted several nearby chain coffee shops.

** Notable Features

**Layout**:
- Front :: Counter, register, pastry case, coffee equipment
- Back :: Small tables, a few comfortable chairs, corner booth
- Side wall :: Community board, outlet bar for laptops
- Window :: Front window with the shop name, looks out on street

**Sensory Details**:
- Sight :: Warm lighting, espresso machine gleaming, people in various states of awakeness
- Sound :: Espresso machine hissing, milk steaming, low conversation, music barely audible
- Smell :: Coffee beans, baked goods, slight burnt-sugar scent from caramel
- Touch :: Smooth counter, warm cup in hand, cool door handle
- Taste :: Quality coffee, better than what Alex makes at home

** Importance in the Plot

**Threshold Space**: Where Alex and Sam's first real conversation happens. Between home and work, between night and day, between chaos and order.

**Character Contrast**:
- For Sam :: Routine (same time, same order every day), peaceful morning ritual
- For Alex (initially) :: Chaos (rushed, line too long, never the same time), stressful rush
- For Alex (finally) :: Peaceful morning ritual like Sam, but on their own terms

**Story Bookends**: First encounter with Sam happens here (Chapter 1). Final scene happens here (Chapter 5), where Alex helps Casey, completing the circle.

** Specific Places

Counter area, seating area, corner booth (possible location for Alex/Sam conversations).

** Atmosphere & Mood

Morning energy. Mix of people waking up and people already energized. Warm, inviting, smells amazing. Background music, espresso machine sounds, low conversation. Comfortable third place.

** Notes

The coffee shop represents routine for Sam and chaos for Alex, then eventually becomes a peaceful ritual for Alex too. It's where change begins and where it comes full circle. Symbolic threshold space.
