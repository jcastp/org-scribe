:Role: Protagonist
:Age: 28
:Gender: Non-binary (they/them)
:Occupation: Freelance graphic designer
:First-appearance: Chapter 1, Scene 1
:END:

** Physical Description

- Height :: 5'7"
- Build :: Average, slightly soft from too many desk hours
- Hair :: Dark brown, medium length, often uncombed
- Eyes :: Brown, with dark circles from poor sleep
- Distinctive features :: Expressive eyebrows, slightly crooked smile, usually has coffee stains somewhere on their clothing

** Personality

- Main traits :: Creative, disorganized, well-meaning, adaptable, self-aware
- Strengths :: Empathetic, intelligent, capable of growth, sense of humor about their flaws
- Weaknesses :: Poor time management, avoidant, perfectionist tendencies, difficulty with routine
- Fears :: Being mediocre, letting people down, being trapped in monotony
- Desire :: To have their life "together" and feel in control
- Need :: To learn that structure can create freedom, not limit it
- Psychological Flaw :: Believes they work better in chaos (they don't)
- Moral Flaw :: Can be self-centered when stressed, forgetting how their chaos affects others

** Background

- Family :: Raised by grandmother after parents divorced; grandmother passed 2 years ago
- Education :: BFA in Graphic Design
- Occupation :: Freelance graphic designer (works from home, which doesn't help the routine issue)
- Formative events :: Grandmother's death left them adrift; losing a major client 6 months ago due to missed deadline; childhood of moving between parents' houses created instability

** Goal, Motivation, Conflict
*** Internal
- Goal :: Find peace and stability in daily life
- Motivation :: Tired of constant stress and feeling like a mess
- Conflict :: Fear that structure will kill creativity; resistance to change; self-sabotage
*** External
- Goal :: Establish a sustainable morning routine
- Motivation :: Keep making the same mistakes (late to meetings, forgot important things)
- Conflict :: Old habits are deeply ingrained; life keeps throwing curveballs; no accountability

** Character Arc

- Initial state :: Chaotic, reactive, running on constant anxiety, blaming circumstances
- Turning point :: Sam's kindness and the watch symbol plant a seed; hitting rock bottom with the meeting disaster
- Transformation :: Learns that routines can be flexible; discovers morning peace; becomes someone who helps others
- Final state :: Balanced, proactive, maintains routine while staying creative, pays forward Sam's kindness

** Relationships

- With Sam :: Starts as strangers, becomes mentor/mentee, evolves to genuine friendship
- With Grandmother (deceased) :: Deep love and admiration; regrets not learning from her earlier; the watch keeps her memory alive
- With themselves :: Journey from self-criticism to self-compassion

** Notes

Character voice: Internal monologue is self-deprecating, slightly sarcastic, observant. Dialogue is honest, sometimes flustered, uses humor as defense. Example: "Close enough" about the socks - shows their resigned acceptance of chaos.

- Weaknesses :: Can be too rigid sometimes, struggles with spontaneity
- Fears :: Falling back into chaos, not being able to help

- Family :: Not detailed in story
- Education :: College degree (field unspecified)
- Occupation :: Project manager at tech company
- Formative events :: Quarter-life crisis 4 years ago; was chaotic like Alex; over-corrected with extreme routines; gradually found balance

** Goal, Motivation, Conflict
*** Internal
- Goal :: Maintain his hard-won balance
- Motivation :: Remembers how difficult the transformation was
- Conflict :: Fear of becoming rigid; tendency to over-structure
*** External
- Goal :: Help Alex without being pushy
- Motivation :: Recognizes himself in Alex's struggles
- Conflict :: Knows he can't force change; must let Alex find their own way

** Character Arc

- Initial state :: Already balanced, serves as mentor
- Turning point :: Realizes Alex needs flexibility, not rigidity
- Transformation :: Learns to adapt his advice to the individual
- Final state :: More accepting of different approaches, witnesses Alex pay it forward

** Relationships

- With Alex :: Neighborly acquaintance → mentor → friend. Sees potential in Alex and genuinely wants to help
- With his past self :: Has compassion for who he was, uses that to help Alex

** Notes

Voice: Calm, measured, uses questions instead of commands. Never condescending, speaks from experience not superiority. Example: "I used to be exactly like you" - establishes connection, not hierarchy.

BETA READER FEEDBACK: Sam feels too perfect. Added flaws: can be too rigid, struggles with spontaneity, occasionally preachy. Backstory shows he overcompensated initially, making him more relatable.
